# SW Urban Solutions Website Generation Prompt

Use the following prompt in ChatGPT, Claude, Lovable, v0, or another AI website builder.

## Prompt

You are designing and writing a professional services website for a transportation consulting firm.

Create a polished, responsive, multi-page website for **SW Urban Solutions**, a **women- and minority-owned, DBE-certified, Minnesota-based transportation consulting firm**. The site should feel established, technically credible, procurement-ready, and agency-friendly. Avoid generic startup, SaaS, or software-product language. This is a professional consulting website focused on qualifications, experience, and technical capability.

## Primary Goal

Build a website that clearly showcases the firm's qualifications, technical expertise, and project experience to:

1. Public agencies, especially MnDOT and local agencies
2. Major consultant firms seeking a qualified subconsultant or teaming partner

The website should communicate that SW Urban Solutions is a trusted, capable transportation consulting partner with strong experience in public-sector transportation planning, modeling, construction impact analysis, freeway operations, and selected transportation design support.

## Audience Priority

Treat public agencies as the primary audience. Prime consultants are the secondary audience.

## Brand and Positioning

Use **SW Urban Solutions** as the company name.

The site should prominently communicate the following qualifications:

- Women- and minority-owned firm
- DBE-certified firm
- Minnesota-based firm
- Pre-qualified to perform MnDOT work through pre-qualification direct select service
- Founder is a Professional Engineer in Minnesota and Washington
- Founder has a great working relationship with MnDOT and Met Council, work reputation high 
- Founder has 16+ years of transportation experience

Include trust-building positioning in a professional, evidence-based tone:

- The founder has built long-term trust with MnDOT through years of successful project support
- The founder's work has been widely recognized for technical quality, practical judgment, and dependable delivery

Do not make the copy sound exaggerated. Phrase credibility in a measured, professional way suitable for public-sector audiences.

## Placeholders

Keep the following items as placeholders:

- Founder name
- Founder headshot
- Founder credentials line
- Email address
- Phone number
- Office address
- Logo
- Certification details if exact wording or numbers are not yet available
- Project photos, maps, or diagrams

## Required Site Structure

Create these pages:

1. Home
2. Services
3. Project Experience
4. About / Qualifications
5. Contact

## Required Output

Generate all of the following:

- Page-by-page site structure
- Section-by-section recommendations for each page
- Professional website copy with headlines, subheads, body copy, and calls to action
- Recommended service descriptions based on the founder's background
- Project cards or short case-study summaries for past work
- A visual direction for the website
- Suggestions for homepage messaging hierarchy

## Tone and Style

Use a tone that is:

- Professional
- Technical but readable
- Concise
- Credible
- Public-sector appropriate
- Confident without sounding promotional or inflated

Avoid:

- Generic marketing filler
- Startup buzzwords
- Overstated claims
- Casual language
- "Data analytics" positioning that makes the firm sound like a general BI company

## Design Direction

The visual design should feel like a transportation infrastructure consulting firm, not a software startup or law office.

Use a refined professional style with:

- Strong typography
- Clean information hierarchy
- Agency-friendly layout
- Structured service and qualifications sections
- Subtle infrastructure or mapping-inspired visual language
- Responsive design for desktop and mobile

The homepage should make the firm's qualifications visible near the top of the page. The design should support credibility, clarity, and selection-readiness.

## Homepage Requirements

The homepage should include:

- A hero section with a clear positioning statement
- A short trust and qualifications band near the top
- A concise overview of service offerings
- A featured experience section
- A featured project section
- A section tailored to public agencies
- A section tailored to prime consultants / teaming partners
- Clear calls to action

The hero or qualifications area should include short trust-building statements such as:

- Trusted by MnDOT through long-term collaboration
- Recognized for reliable, technically sound transportation analysis

- Socioeconomic data analysis

- Dynamic assignment analysis

State that the founder worked for a Metropolitan Planning Organization for more than five years.

- Metro District construction traffic impact analysis
- Construction preferred staging analysis
- Freeway operation analysis
- Traffic Signal optimization
- Local traffic impact analysis due to construction
- Construction impact mitigation
- Construction schedule recommendations
- Traffic control design
- Pavement marking design
- Leading traffic model development using an activity-based model
- Developing user-friendly tools for construction and maintenance staff to evaluate lane closure impacts and recommend duration and timing
- Regional Transportation Management Center performance measurement work
- E-ZPass / MnPASS speed study support
- Statewide data acquisition implementation
- Real-time and historical speed data acquisition
- Travel behavior inventory acquisition

### 3. Local Government and Transportation Design Support

Reflect experience working with cities and local agencies on:

- Transportation project development
- Residential development and site planning support
- Presenting roadway design alternatives to city council and the public
- Transportation infrastructure design support
- Traffic signage
- Striping
- ADA sidewalk considerations
- Access design
- Site layout considerations to meet city code and standards

## Messaging Requirements

The copy should clearly communicate:

- Technical depth in transportation planning and operations
- Construction impact and staging expertise
- Experience supporting MnDOT and local agencies
- Ability to support both public agencies and larger consultant teams

## Content Quality Requirements

Make the content feel specific to transportation consulting in the Upper Midwest and public-sector project delivery. The copy should feel credible to MnDOT, local agencies, and prime consultant firms.

Do not write generic placeholder copy like:

- "We provide innovative solutions"
- "We leverage data-driven insights"
- "We help clients unlock value"

Instead, write grounded, concrete copy tied to transportation planning, operations, modeling, construction impacts, and implementation support.
