Work zone modeling

SP 2750-85 | TH 169 from 101st Ave to Hayden Lake Rd

Used Synchro and SimTraffic to evaluate northbound lane closure impacts, delay, and queue risk during pavement rehabilitation.

  • Challenge: understand NB TH 169 lane closure effects through a signal-controlled section without direct freeway detector coverage.
  • Approach: built a Synchro and SimTraffic model to test afternoon peak delay and queue risk during rehabilitation work.
  • Value: helped define when impacts would be most severe and supported construction planning with modeled operations insight.

Combined MnDOT loop detector volumes with StreetLight origin-destination data to recommend which ramps could close concurrently.

  • Challenge: identify ramp closure combinations that would minimize corridor disruption during planned work.
  • Approach: paired MnDOT loop volumes with StreetLight origin-destination data to isolate the most critical ramp movements.
  • Value: turned raw travel-pattern data into clear ramp closure recommendations for project delivery.
